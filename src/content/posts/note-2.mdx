Some people walk into your life and quietly make it better.  
These are the people in my life who deserve to be seen.  
Not just by me, but by anyone reading this.  
This series is my way of saying thank you.  
Not loudly. Just honestly.

## [Vedang Deshpande](https://www.instagram.com/iamvedangd/)
<b>Explorer. Guide. The colleague who became everything else.</b>

I've had many colleagues.  
I don't remember most of them.  
Vedang I won't forget.  
Colleague on paper. Brother in everything else.

<Youtube id='VC66N4VJ8cw' />

Yes, that guy struggling with the ice cream. That's him.

He is smart.  
A traveller.  
A photo addict but strictly of himself.  
Always has a smile on his face.  
Always there when you need him.

The kind of person who genuinely believes  
you should try everything in life.  
Not tag it good or bad, not overthink it, just experience it.  
And he doesn't just say that,  
he has travelled the world, done things most people only talk about,  
and come back every time with a story worth hearing.

If you ever need a trip planned, call Vedang.

I've travelled with many people.  
But I've travelled most with him.  
And every trip has been different —  
not just a different place,  
but a different kind of experience.  
He has this thing where he refuses to do the same trip twice.  
Every journey has to leave you with something new.

---

One day, out of nowhere, he asked me about North Indian treks in the Himalayas.

I said yes.

And it was the best decision of my life.  
I would never have considered that trip on my own.

Never!

But he encouraged me, planned it, made it happen —  
and what I experienced there is something I will carry for the rest of my life.  
The mountains, the silence, the scale of it all.  
I saw nature in a way I didn't know was possible.  
That trip changed something in me.  
And it exists only because Vedang asked a random question one afternoon.

He has also been there professionally.  
Always suggesting things that will actually help you grow —  
not generic advice, not textbook answers,  
but real corporate lessons from someone who has navigated it himself.  
That kind of guidance is rare.  
Especially from someone who was just supposed to be a colleague.

Being a Deshpande, he is strictly vegetarian.  
Every life matters to him. He does not support cruelty.  
He made this very clear to all of us.

He is the kind of person who takes hospitality seriously.  
When I visited his place, I was welcomed with a proper meal.  
Panch pakwan, complete mehmaan nawazi.

---

He left the office last year.  
And the floor hasn't felt the same since.  
The chaos, the conversations, the random plans   
all of it gone with one job change.   
I miss it more than I expected to.  

A good friend who shows up.  
A good footballer on the field.  
A funny guy everywhere else.  
That's Vedang in three lines.  

This was all about him…

---

People like Vedang are rare.  
The kind who show up not just for the fun trips and the good days,  
but for the career confusion, the bad Mondays, the moments when you need someone to just tell you the truth.  
I never expected to find that in a colleague.  
Most of the time you don't.  
But every once in a while, if you're lucky,  
someone walks through the same office door as you  
and ends up being one of the most important people in your life.

I got lucky.  
Vedang is that person for me.  
And I'm grateful.  
More than this post can fully say.
