Some people walk into your life and quietly make it better.  
These are the people in my life who deserve to be seen.  
Not just by me, but by anyone reading this.  
This series is my way of saying thank you.  
Not loudly. Just honestly.

## [Sahil Talwad](https://www.instagram.com/sahiltalwad/)
<b>Jolly. Caring. The kind of friend every college group needs.</b>

![Sa_hill](/assets/images/sahil/Sahil.jpg)

This cruel man who finished two full fish without a single moment of hesitation or regret. That's him.

I've had many friends in life.  
But some friendships have a specific origin story.  
This one starts in 2016, first year of college, when a jolly Punjabi guy walked into my life and never really left.

That's Sahil.

He is the kind of person who lights up a room without trying.  
Caring, warm, the unofficial big brother of every group he's ever been part of.  
The kind of person who just naturally takes care of everyone around him without being asked, without keeping score.

Also the only Punjabi I know who lost a Punjabi language debate to someone from Pune.  
The details are classified.

---

He was one of those people who somehow had everything figured out.

Assignments? Done.

Presentations? Prepared.

Exams? Already revised.

And somehow, despite all of that,  
he was still the funniest person in the room.

I don't know how people like that exist.  
I just know college became a lot easier because one of them was my friend.

After every exam, my first call was Sahil.

Not to celebrate.

Not to complain.

Just to ask,

*"Did I write this answer correctly?"*

He always knew.

Which was comforting...

and slightly irritating at the same time.

Once college ends, most friendships quietly fade.  
The daily conversations become weekly.  
Weekly becomes monthly.  
Monthly becomes one call a year, if you're lucky.

Sahil never let that happen.

We still meet. We still talk.  
He still gives advice. He still shows up when I need something.  
Nobody wrote a rule that said he had to.  
He just does.

That kind of friendship is rare.  
The kind that doesn't need proximity or routine to survive.  
It just continues, quietly, because it's real.


This was all about him...

---

People like Sahil are rare.

The jolly ones who make every room a little brighter.

The brilliant ones who never make you feel small.

The friends who quietly become older brothers  
without either of you ever saying it out loud.

Life has changed a lot since 2016.

Different jobs.

Different responsibilities.

Different versions of ourselves.

But somehow,  
our friendship never really changed.

And maybe that's the best kind of friendship there is.

I'm grateful that a jolly Punjabi guy  
walked into my life during my first year of college.

Because somewhere along the way,

he stopped being just another classmate.

He became family.
