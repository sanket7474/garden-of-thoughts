**Notes to Self — When In Doubt, Zoom Out**
 
Notes to Self is exactly what it sounds like things I'm telling myself when life feels heavier than it probably is.  
No polish, no pretending to have it figured out.  
Just honest thoughts, written down so future me can come back and remember.

---

There's a feeling I've been sitting with lately.  
Like I'm behind.  
Like everyone else is moving and I'm standing still.  
Like I lack something — skill, discipline, whatever you want to call it.

I sit down to study, and somehow the hours just slip.  
Not because I decided to waste them.  
It just happens.  
A phone picked up without thinking. A scroll that turns into thirty minutes. A "five more minutes" that becomes the whole evening.

I don't know if it's lack of motivation or lack of focus.  
Maybe they're the same thing wearing different clothes.

---

And underneath all of it, there's this constant comparison.

Someone's switching jobs. Someone's already at the company I want to be at. Someone's learned in six months what's taken me longer.

And the mind does what it always does — it skips straight to the worst conclusion.  
*I'm behind. I lack the skills. I'm not good enough.*

Some of that might be true.  
But probably not at the scale my head is making it out to be.

That's the thing about comparison — it never shows you the full picture. Just the highlight reel of someone else's life next to the rough draft of yours.

---

I found this image the other day, and it stopped me for a second.

![When in doubt, just zoom out](/assets/images/notes-to-self/zoom-out.jpg)

Two graphs. Same data. Different zoom.

The first one — a small decline. A dip after a peak. If that's all you're looking at, it feels like everything is falling apart.

The second one — the same exact dip, but zoomed out. Suddenly it's not a fall. It's a tiny wobble in a line that's been climbing for a long time.

That's exactly what this feels like.

I look at where I am right now — distracted, inconsistent, comparing myself to people further ahead — and it feels like a decline. Like I'm losing ground.

But if I actually zoom out, if I look at where I started and where I am now, it's not a decline at all. It's a small dip in a much longer climb. One that's still going up, even if it doesn't feel like it some days.

---

If you're feeling this way too — like you're behind, like you lack something, like everyone else has it figured out — I want to say this clearly.

It's okay to feel that way.

It doesn't mean something is wrong with you. It just means you're paying attention to your life, which is more than most people do. The discomfort is uncomfortable, but it's not dangerous. It's just a signal.

Maybe the real shift isn't about catching up to anyone.

Stop comparing yourself to the person sitting next to you, the one whose timeline you don't actually know. Compare yourself to who you were a year ago instead. Are you a little wiser? A little calmer? Do you handle the same situations a little better than you used to?

That's the only comparison that's actually fair, because it's the only one with the full picture.

Being satisfied doesn't mean you stop growing. It means you stop measuring your worth against someone else's pace. It means noticing the version of you from last year, and being okay — even proud — of the gap between then and now, however small it is.

That's enough. That can be enough.

---

So here's what I'm committing to. Not as a fix-everything-overnight plan, just small things I can actually stick to.

**A morning routine.** Nothing extreme. Just something that starts the day with intention instead of a scroll.

**Less screen time.** Not zero, just less. Less mindless, more on purpose.

**More reading.** Something that builds instead of consumes.

**Being happy with where I am, while still moving forward.** Not settling. Just not punishing myself on the way there.

That's it. Small, repeatable, boring even. But boring is sustainable. And sustainable is what actually moves the line upward over time.

---

---

So maybe this is just a note to myself, more than anything else.

The dip is real. The distraction is real. The lack of consistency is real.  
But it doesn't erase everything that came before it.

I don't need to fix everything today.  
I just need to zoom out enough to remember I'm still climbing.

And maybe that's enough for now.
